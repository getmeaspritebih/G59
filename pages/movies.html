Music

Kill Yourself (Part III)

Part 3 of the “Kill Yourself” singles series. In this track, Ruby talks battling the depression that was triggered by his girlfriend of 5 years breaking up with him. $crim, however, was battling his addiction to opiates and his depression.

O PANA!

“O PANA!” perfectly translates $UICIDEBOY$‘ excessive drug use. The beat, created by $crim, was inspired by fellow rapper Lil Ugly Mane—whom is also famous for songs with dark beats and heavy lyrics which [usually] depict nefarious characteristics. The title, “O Pana!” relates to a powerful narcotic containing Oxymorphone but is also spelt in a way to reference Walt Whitman’s famous poem, “Pioneers! O Pioneers!”. This was confirmed by Ruby in an interview with Genius. “O Pana!” was released in 2016 in their album Eternal Grey and instantly became one of the more notable songs from the album, amassing over ten-million views combined from the two most popular videos uploaded to YouTube.

Paris

As the opener to the legendary Now The Moon’s Rising, the equally as legendary Paris was a standout song in what fans have dubbed, the golden era of the boy$. It was one of the songs responsible for blowing up the boys in the underground scene.
